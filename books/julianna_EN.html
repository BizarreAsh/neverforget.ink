I
Flowers to Someone Else's Address

A post scrolled by in the feed. A card with text — "I want a surprise flower delivery" — and at the bottom, in small letters, almost like a whisper: "p.s. wanting doesn't hurt." Ash read it. Smiled. Thought for a second.

He had something to do with flowers at the time — work, connections, a couple of calls and a few button presses. It wasn't a grand gesture — just the ability to do something nice for someone he'd barely met. Or almost barely.

A surprise delivery meant one thing — it had to be a surprise. You call the recipient only when the courier is already at the door. Ash wrote to her friend — persuasively, with reasons, asked her not to give him away and to share an address where it would be convenient to receive an unexpected delivery. The friend hesitated — and gave it up. The flowers went.

· · ·

Ju received them where it suited her — and received them in full. Not just flowers, but everything behind them — the unexpectedness, the attention, the feeling that someone had read that post and hadn't been too lazy to act. She wrote back. They started talking.

Out of nothing — out of a card in a feed and one well-made phone call — appeared a person who turned out to be closer in interests than many of those Ash had known for years.

· · ·
II
Ju

The nickname stuck on its own — from the username Julilit88, without effort, it just became that and stayed. Ju.

She was an artist — in the way artists are when they can't be anything else. She created beauty and warmth everywhere she appeared — not because she was trying, simply because that's how she was made. Her hands naturally reached for something visual, for colour, for form.

Genshin Impact occupied a separate and unshakeable place in her life. Ash thought that if the game ever shut down, you might lose the person along with it. He played for a while himself — interesting at first, then the standard story: either grind for hours or pay. That didn't stop Ju. She played — with the kind of devotion that doesn't need to be explained logically, just because.

· · ·

Minecraft became their shared territory. Ash tried to explain the technology, show how the mechanisms worked, why a nuclear reactor mattered. Ju listened — and went to build the design around that same reactor. Beautiful, considered, with details Ash would never have noticed. How the reactor works — not important. What matters is that the space around it feels right.

They didn't get in each other's way. Each did their own thing — and together it added up to something alive. That happens when two people complement each other not because they're trying to, but simply because that's how it works out.

Her character had its angles — inconsistency, a habit of disappearing and reappearing on her own schedule. Known and accepted. Because everything else outweighed it.

· · ·
III
The Partner

Ash had never had a partner for two-player games — the specific kind where you need a particular person beside you, not just anyone. Ju turned out to be exactly that person. With the right one — games of that genre play in completely different colours. It's not about skill and not about results — it's about having someone nearby who's actually interested.

They didn't try to build a relationship. Nobody announced this out loud — it just worked out that way. They were friends, partners, people who were good in the same space. Each had their own life — that didn't get in anyone's way. They were happy spending time together without translating it into something else.

That's rare — finding someone with whom everything is just simple. Not because there are no complications, but because the complications don't outweigh the good.

· · ·

Then Ju disappeared. Not left — disappeared. Messages stay unread. No explanation, no final conversation, just at some point she stopped answering.

That was her way — Ash knew it from the start. She always lived on her own schedule, appeared and vanished when she felt like it. A difficult character — but her own, genuine.

Most likely she's fine. She's just not answering right now.

Ju — if you ever read this — the design around that reactor was the best thing in the game. You know that.
